# Story 2 – Fixed Cost Absorption

## Situation

During a factory review, I noticed that product costs were increasing despite stable operational spending.

Management initially believed that manufacturing costs were out of control.

---

## Task

I needed to identify the real driver behind the cost increase and explain the financial impact to management.

---

## Actions

I analyzed production volume, fixed cost allocation, and capacity utilization across production lines.

I compared actual production levels against standard capacity assumptions and evaluated the impact on fixed cost absorption.

---

## Result

The analysis showed that the increase in unit cost was primarily caused by lower production volume rather than higher spending.

This helped management understand that the issue was a capacity utilization problem rather than a cost control problem.

The finding enabled more effective operational actions and prevented incorrect cost reduction initiatives.

---

## Key Interview Message

I focus on identifying root causes rather than reporting symptoms.
