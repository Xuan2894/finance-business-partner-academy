# Standard Costing System Across 6 Production Cost Centres

## Situation

The company lacked a standardized costing structure across multiple production cost centres.

As a result, cost visibility was limited and commercial teams had difficulty understanding the true drivers of product costs.

---

## Action

By embedding myself in the factory for a month alongside operators and the management team, I was able to map key production processes and operational drivers into the ERP system.

I worked closely with production, planning, engineering, and finance teams to establish a standardized costing methodology across six production cost centres.

---

## Enablement

With detailed routing, machine time, and capacity information captured in the ERP system, we could gain full visibility into production capacity and resource utilization.

With a standardized costing structure in place, commercial and operational teams could work from a single version of the truth when evaluating cost performance.

---

## Business Impact

This enabled the business to distinguish whether cost variances were driven by technical processes or capacity utilization issues.

As a result, the company could make more informed pricing decisions, improve quotation accuracy, and better protect gross margins.
