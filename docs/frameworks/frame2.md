# Frame 2 – Action → Impact

## Structure

By doing X, I was able to Y.

"By doing [specific action], I was able to [concrete business outcome]."
"By doing [specific action], I gave [stakeholder] the ability to [outcome]."
"By doing [specific action], the business was able to [outcome]."
---

## Why It Works

This structure highlights ownership and business impact.

Instead of describing activities, it shows what was achieved.

---

## Examples

By automating the monthly reporting process, I was able to reduce preparation time by 80%.

By improving cost allocation logic, I was able to increase costing accuracy across all product categories.

By embedding myself in the factory, I was able to identify operational inefficiencies and improve cost visibility.

---

## My Story

By embedding myself in the factory for a month alongside operators and the management team, I was able to map key production processes into the ERP system.

By doing so, I was able to provide detailed visibility into factory capacity and machine utilization, supporting more informed commercial and operational decisions.
